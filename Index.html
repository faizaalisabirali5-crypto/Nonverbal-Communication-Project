About Nonverbal Communication

Nonverbal communication is communication without words. People use facial expressions, gestures, posture, and eye contact to express emotions and feelings.

Understanding body language helps improve relationships, trust, and communication skills.

Facial Expressions

😊

Happiness

A smile usually shows friendliness, comfort, and positivity.

😠

Anger

An angry face may show frustration, stress, or disagreement.

😢

Sadness

Sad expressions can communicate disappointment or emotional pain.

😨

Nervousness

Nervous expressions may show fear, anxiety, or discomfort.

Body Language

Crossed Arms

This may show defensiveness, discomfort, or disagreement.

Open Posture

An open posture usually shows confidence and friendliness.

Leaning Forward

This often shows interest and active listening.

Looking Away

Looking away may suggest nervousness or lack of attention.

Eye Contact

Good Eye Contact

Good eye contact can build trust, confidence, and stronger communication.

No Eye Contact

Avoiding eye contact may make someone appear nervous or distracted.

Personal Space

Close Distance

Close personal space is common between family members and close friends.

Comfortable Distance

Comfortable distance helps people feel relaxed during conversation.

Too Close

Standing too close may make someone uncomfortable.

Nonverbal communication affects our daily interactions and relationships. Understanding body language helps people communicate more clearly, respectfully, and confidently.
